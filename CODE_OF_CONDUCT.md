# Code of Conduct

## ü§ù Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## üåü Our Standards

### ‚úÖ Examples of Behavior that Contributes to a Positive Environment

- **Being Respectful**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Being Empathetic**: Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- **Giving Constructive Feedback**: Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- **Focusing on Community**: Focusing on what is best for the overall community
- **Showing Gratitude**: Showing empathy and kindness toward other community members

### ‚ùå Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassment**: Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- **Privacy Violations**: Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Inappropriate Content**: Sexual language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- **Professional Misconduct**: Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- **Disrespect**: Dismissing or attacking inclusion-oriented requests

## üõ°Ô∏è Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## üö® Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by contacting:

**üìß Email**: zoro@alphagodeye.com

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### What to Include

When reporting, please include:

1. **Your contact information**
2. **Names of individuals involved** (or pseudonyms if preferred)
3. **Description of the behavior** (be as specific as possible)
4. **Date and time** (or approximate timeframe)
5. **Location/context** (GitHub issue, PR, chat, etc.)
6. **Any supporting evidence** (screenshots, links, etc.)
7. **Any witnesses** (if applicable)

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion. We will respect confidentiality requests for the purpose of protecting victims of abuse.

## ‚öñÔ∏è Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. üì¢ Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. ‚ö†Ô∏è Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. ‚è∏Ô∏è Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. üö´ Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## üìö Additional Guidelines

### For Contributors

- **Be Patient**: Not everyone has the same level of experience
- **Be Helpful**: Answer questions constructively
- **Be Professional**: Keep discussions technical and respectful
- **Be Open-Minded**: Different perspectives can lead to better solutions
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together towards common goals

### For Maintainers

- **Be Fair**: Apply rules consistently to everyone
- **Be Transparent**: Explain decisions when appropriate
- **Be Responsive**: Address reports promptly
- **Be Supportive**: Foster a welcoming environment
- **Be Accountable**: Set a good example for the community

### For Code Reviewers

- **Focus on the Code**: Review code, not the person
- **Be Constructive**: Explain why changes are needed
- **Be Respectful**: Acknowledge effort and good work
- **Be Specific**: Provide clear, actionable feedback
- **Be Timely**: Review PRs in a reasonable timeframe

## üí¨ Communication Guidelines

### GitHub Issues & PRs

- **Be Clear**: Use descriptive titles and detailed descriptions
- **Be Respectful**: Disagree with ideas, not people
- **Stay On Topic**: Keep discussions relevant to the issue/PR
- **Use Reactions**: Use emoji reactions instead of "+1" comments
- **Search First**: Check if your issue already exists

### Discussions & Forums

- **Be Welcoming**: Greet newcomers warmly
- **Be Helpful**: Share knowledge generously
- **Be Patient**: Everyone learns at their own pace
- **Be Positive**: Encourage and support others
- **Be Professional**: Maintain a constructive tone

### Code Reviews

- **Assume Good Intent**: Contributors are doing their best
- **Ask Questions**: "What do you think about..." instead of "You should..."
- **Offer Solutions**: Suggest alternatives when pointing out issues
- **Praise Good Work**: Acknowledge well-written code
- **Be Timely**: Review within 2-3 business days when possible
